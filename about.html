Our Story
The Association of Engineers, India, was established by a group of committed nationalist Indian engineers in the year of 1919, during the British Raj, to strength professional fraternity among Indian Engineers, and to foster sustained professional ethics among them. Registered under " West Bengal Societies Registration Act 1961. The Institute is now poised to make its programme five-fold based on Education, Training, Consultancy and Inter- Institutional collaboration. Our Association has been promoting various knowledge upgrading and skill developing courses and training programme for engineers and technically qualified persons from weaker section of society. The Association is also engaged in imparting job oriented training to personnel in various disciplines of engineering and trades. The publication of the association, such as the Journal, number of book and manuals on engineering and related areas are widely acclaimed.


The Association of Engineers, India is a landmark at Bidhannagar (Salt Lake City), close to Yuva Bharati Stadium of Salt Lake City, on the outskirts of Kolkata.


The Institute has good facilities for education and training which include audio-visual aids and a library. It also well connected to Railway Station, Airport, Bus-terminus.


The Association has amongst its members all blends of technical personalities from academics and organizations.


The Association has its own “Rooftop Rain Water Harvesting System” and small “Rooftop Solar Photovoltaic Power Generation System”.
